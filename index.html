Column Guide — What Everything Means

Hover any column header in the tables for a quick tooltip

Status & Identification

STATUS
Probability of index change at the next rebalance.
⬆ STRONG — ranked inside the add buffer, meets all criteria. High probability of being added.
⬇ STRONG — ranked outside the delete buffer. High probability of removal.
◆ POSS — within ~25 ranks of the buffer. Watch closely — could move in or out.
○ WATCH — further from buffer but worth monitoring, especially if price is moving.
TICKER
ASX ticker code. M&A tag means an acquisition has been announced — this stock is ineligible for index promotion from announcement date per S&P methodology.
COMPANY
Company name. Click any row to expand the full detail panel.

Market Cap & Ranking

FF MKT CAP (Float-Adjusted Market Cap)
The primary ranking metric S&P uses. Calculated as:
Price × Shares on Issue × IWF (free float factor)
S&P uses the 3-month daily average. This tool uses live market cap from Yahoo Finance as a real-time proxy. The two will differ slightly — spot mkt cap moves daily while S&P's average smooths out volatility.

Example: A$2.5b means the stock ranks by a $2.5 billion float-adjusted market cap.
RANK
The stock's current rank by FF Mkt Cap among all eligible stocks. Lower number = larger = higher ranked.

The coloured bar shows position relative to the buffer boundary:
Green = inside add buffer (non-constituent) or inside del buffer (constituent)
Amber = near the boundary
Red = outside deletion buffer

Buffer rules: ASX 200 adds stocks ranked ≤179, removes ≥221. ASX 300 adds ≤274, removes ≥326.
IWF (Investable Weight Factor)
Free float fraction used in FMC calculation. A stock 100% publicly tradeable = IWF 1.0. A stock with 40% insider/strategic holdings = IWF ~0.6. Minimum IWF of 0.15 required for index entry.

For dual-listed stocks (BHP, RIO, AMC etc.), Yahoo may use global share count rather than ASX CDI count — this can make their FMC appear much larger than the ASX-specific reality.

Price & Liquidity

LIVE PRICE
Current market price from Yahoo Finance (15-20 min delayed). The percentage below shows today's change vs prior close.
+1.2% = up today    −0.8% = down today
RL% (Relative Liquidity)
A stock's trading activity relative to the market average. Calculated as:
RL = (Stock's MDVT ÷ Stock's FMC) ÷ Market average × 100

This is a hard gate — a stock must pass this to be eligible, regardless of rank:
≥50% required for ASX 200 and above
≥30% required for ASX 300
25-50% = at risk of removal from ASX 200
<25% = below removal threshold — will be removed

Note: this tool uses on-market volume only. S&P includes off-market block trades, so actual RL will be slightly higher than shown here.
MDVT (Median Daily Value Traded)
The median dollar value of shares traded per day over the past 3 months. Shown in A$m.

Example: MDVT of A$8.5m means on a typical day, $8.5 million of this stock changes hands.

Used to calculate RL% and DTC. Higher MDVT = more liquid = easier for passive funds to trade into.

Passive Flow

FLOW A$m (Estimated Passive Flow)
Estimated dollar value that index-tracking (passive) funds would need to buy or sell if this stock is added or removed at the rebalance. Calculated as:
Passive Flow = (Stock's FMC ÷ Total Index FMC) × Total Passive AUM

+A$24m = passive funds need to BUY ~$24m at the effective date
−A$657m = passive funds need to SELL ~$657m at the effective date

You can adjust the passive AUM assumptions (top right) if you have better estimates. Defaults: ASX 200 = A$80bn, ASX 300 = A$15bn.
DTC (Days to Close)
How many days of that stock's average trading volume the passive flow represents:
DTC = Passive Flow ÷ MDVT (in $)

DTC < 1 = passive flow is less than one day's volume — easily absorbed, low market impact
DTC 1–5 = moderate — passive funds will spread buying/selling over multiple days
DTC > 5 = high impact — passive flow is large relative to typical volume. Active managers can trade ahead of this.

Example: DTC of 53 days means passive funds need to buy 53 days' worth of average volume — highly price sensitive.

Price to Include / Exclude

PRICE TO INCLUDE (additions)
The average price a stock needs to sustain for the remaining days of the reference period to rank inside the addition buffer by the reference date.

Formula: Target FMC × Total Days − FMC already accumulated = FMC still needed ÷ remaining days ÷ (shares × IWF)

−5% from spot = already tracking to qualify — stock needs to fall -5% and it still gets in
+12% from spot = needs a 12% sustained rally from here to qualify
+45% from spot = very unlikely at this stage of the reference period

Important: this is the average price needed, not a one-day target. S&P uses the 3-month average, so today's price matters less than sustained movement.
PRICE TO EXCLUDE (removals)
The average price a current constituent needs to fall to in order to rank outside the deletion buffer by the reference date — i.e. how far it needs to drop to be removed.

Displayed as: A$X.XX (+Y% from spot means it needs to rise this much to be safe)

Key Dates & Rules

Reference Period
The 3-month window S&P uses to calculate average FMC and liquidity. Runs from the 2nd-to-last Friday of the month 4 months before effective date, back 3 months.

For June 2026: ref period runs ~16 Feb 2026 → 22 May 2026 (reference date)
Announcement Date
First Friday of the rebalancing month. S&P announces all additions and deletions. This is when the market reacts — stocks being added typically rally, stocks being removed typically fall.
Effective Date
Third Friday of the rebalancing month. Changes take effect after market close. Passive funds must complete their trades by this date. This is when the actual buying/selling happens — the DTC column shows how many days they have to do it (they can start from announcement).
Buffer Rules
ASX 20: add ≤14th rank, remove ≥26th
ASX 50: add ≤39th rank, remove ≥61st
ASX 100: add ≤84th rank, remove ≥116th
ASX 200: add ≤179th rank, remove ≥221st
ASX 300: add ≤274th rank, remove ≥326th

The Index Committee has discretion to override these, especially when M&A creates a vacancy mid-quarter.
